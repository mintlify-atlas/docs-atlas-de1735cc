Remote Power Button (rpb) is a small Go service that runs on a Raspberry Pi and gives you full control over your desktop's power button from any network. Connect a relay to your motherboard's power button header and a wire to the status LED — then power cycle your PC from a browser or a single `curl` command, from anywhere in the world.

## How it works

rpb connects a Raspberry Pi to two points on your motherboard:

- **Output pin** — drives a relay that shorts the power button header
- **Input pin** — reads the power LED to detect whether the machine is on or off

The Go server exposes an HTTP API protected by HTTP Basic Auth. Every press and state change is recorded to a local SQLite database so you have a full audit trail.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Wire the hardware">
    Connect a relay to the power button header and a wire from the status LED to a GPIO input pin on your Raspberry Pi.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set your secret">
    Copy `.example.env` to `.env` and set `RPB_SECRET` to a strong password.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Run the server">
    Start rpb with `docker compose up -d`. It binds to port **5000** by default.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Press the button">
    Open `http://<pi-address>:5000` in your browser or call `POST /press` from anywhere.
  </Step>
</Steps>
